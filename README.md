# MIS 311 - Supermarket Sales Analysis

## Project Overview

This repository showcases my MIS 311 Assignment #1 project, which focuses on exploratory data analysis of a Supermarket Sales dataset. The analysis examines the dataset structure, checks data quality, summarises key sales characteristics, and identifies meaningful patterns related to supermarket sales performance.

## Dataset

The selected dataset contains supermarket sales transaction records. It includes information about sales ID, supermarket branch, city, customer type, product name, product category, quantity sold, and total sales price.

The main variables used in this analysis are:

- `city`: The city where the supermarket sale occurred
- `customer_type`: The type of customer involved in the transaction
- `product_category`: The category of the product sold
- `quantity`: The number of items sold
- `total_price`: The total sales amount in USD

The original dataset contains 253 sales records and 8 variables. After removing duplicate rows and incomplete records, the final cleaned dataset contains 239 complete records.

## Project Objectives

- Understand the overall structure and context of the dataset
- Check for missing values and duplicate records
- Clean the dataset to improve data quality
- Summarise key numerical variables using descriptive statistics
- Identify two meaningful insights related to supermarket sales performance
- Present findings through clear visualisations

## Key Findings

### Insight 1: Chicago Generated the Highest Total Revenue

Chicago generated the highest total revenue, with total sales of 10,813.94 USD, followed closely by New York with 10,613.69 USD. This shows that Chicago and New York were the strongest sales locations in the dataset. The supermarket can focus more on marketing activities and inventory planning in these cities to improve sales performance.

### Insight 2: Fruits Had the Highest Revenue Among Product Categories

Fruits generated the highest total revenue, with total sales of 7,450.12 USD, followed by Beverages and Stationery. This indicates that Fruits contributed the most to supermarket revenue among all product categories. The supermarket should maintain sufficient stock for Fruits and consider suitable promotions to further increase sales in this category.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how exploratory data analysis can be used to transform supermarket sales data into meaningful business insights. The findings suggest that sales performance differs across cities and product categories. In particular, Chicago and Fruits were the strongest performers in this dataset, which can help guide future marketing, inventory, and sales planning decisions.
